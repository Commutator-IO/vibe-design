# Immersion & Empathy (Discover)

The first diamond: **diverge** to understand the user and context deeply before deciding anything. Go to the field; don't theorize from the desk. Pick a few tools that fit the subject.

## Prepare
- **State of the art** — survey what's already known and published on the subject.
- **Stakeholder map** — map everyone touched by the problem: users, influencers, decision-makers, blockers.
- **Benchmarking** — study comparable solutions inside and outside the sector.
- **Word cloud / mind mapping** — warm up the topic, surface assumptions and sub-questions before going out.

## Go to the field
- **Observation (fly on the wall)** — watch users in their real context without intervening; note what they *do*, not just what they say.
- **Total immersion** — live the user's experience yourself where possible.
- **Surveys / photo journal / guided tour** — surveys for breadth; ask users to document their day in photos; have them walk you through their environment.
- **Expert interviews** — talk to domain experts to sharpen your understanding.

## Interview deeply
- **Interview guide + in-depth interviews** — write an interview guide of open, non-leading questions. Start broad, follow curiosity, ask "why" and "tell me about the last time…". Listen far more than you talk; capture verbatim quotes and emotions.
- **Experience sharing** — collect first-hand accounts of the experience you're designing for.

## Principles
- Talk to real users, not proxies. Chase surprises and pain, not confirmation.
- Capture raw material (quotes, photos, observations) faithfully — the next phase mines it.
- Diverge now: gather more than feels necessary. You'll converge in `analysis`.

## Exit checklist
Stakeholders mapped · prior art and benchmarks reviewed · users observed and interviewed in context · raw quotes/photos/notes captured. Proceed to `analysis`.
