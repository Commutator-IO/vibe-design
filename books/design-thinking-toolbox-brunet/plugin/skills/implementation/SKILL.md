# Implementation (Deliver — make it real)

Turn a validated concept into something the organization can actually ship, fund, and scale. This closes the second diamond: converge on a viable path.

## Test the business logic
- **SWOT** — assess strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, threats of the concept.
- **Value proposition canvas** — align the solution's pain-relievers and gain-creators to the persona's jobs, pains, and gains.
- **Business Model Canvas** — lay out how the concept creates, delivers, and captures value (segments, channels, revenue, costs, partners…). Confirms **viable** and **desirable** hold together.

## Define success and plan
- **SSMAART objectives** — set objectives that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, Time-bound (and Awesome/ambitious). Define what success looks like before building.
- **Action plan (roadmap)** — sequence the work from POC → MVP → MAP into concrete milestones.
- **Resource plan** — the people, budget, and tools required.
- **The future team** — identify who owns and runs it after the workshop ends.

## Sell and scale
- **Pitch & storytelling** — craft a compelling narrative of the problem, the user, and the solution to win sponsors and users. Relate the real journey, no fiction.
- **Grow and scale** — plan how the solution scales beyond the pilot.
- **From design thinking to agile, lean, growth hacking** — hand the validated concept to delivery methods (agile/lean) and growth practices for execution.

## Principles
- Don't lose the user in the spreadsheet — every business choice traces back to the persona's jobs and pains.
- Ship the smallest awesome version (MAP), learn in market, then scale.

## Exit checklist
Value proposition + business model validated · success defined (SSMAART) · roadmap, resources, and owning team set · pitch ready · scaling and delivery-method hand-off planned. The concept is ready to build for real (see the `build-sprint` or `shape-up` plugins).
