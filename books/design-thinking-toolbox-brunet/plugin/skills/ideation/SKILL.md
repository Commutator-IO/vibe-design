# Ideation (Develop — diverge)

Open the second diamond: **diverge** to generate many ideas against the reframed challenge, then **converge** to a shortlist. Separate the two — never judge while generating.

## Warm up
- **7 méthodes d'échauffement cérébral** — quick creative warm-ups to loosen the group and break routine thinking before serious ideation.

## Diverge — generate widely
Run one or more, always tied to the "How Might We" challenge:
- **Brainstorming 6-3-5** (+ variants) — 6 people write 3 ideas in 5 minutes, then pass and build on each other's. Structured, silent-start generation that beats a free-for-all.
- **Fleur de lotus** (lotus flower) — put the challenge at the center, derive 8 themes around it, then 8 ideas per theme. Forces breadth.
- **Se mettre dans la peau de…** — ideate as someone else (a child, a competitor, a celebrity) to escape your defaults.
- **Mash-up** — combine two unrelated concepts to spark novel solutions.
- **Mood board** — assemble visual inspiration to fuel ideas.
- **World Café / jeu de rôle d'idéation** — rotating small-group conversations and role-play to build ideas collectively.

Rules: defer judgment, go for quantity, welcome wild ideas, build on others ("yes, and"), one idea per note, stay visual.

## Converge — select
- **Vote par gommettes** (dot voting) — each person spends a few dots on the ideas they believe in; patterns emerge fast.
- **Avocat de l'ange** (angel's advocate) — the opposite of devil's advocate: actively argue *for* fragile promising ideas so they aren't killed too early.
- **Quick Wins — idées vertes / idées noires** — sort ideas by impact vs. effort; "green" ideas are easy wins, "black" are hard/risky. Pick a balanced shortlist.

## Principles
- Generate alone, then share (avoids anchoring and loudest-voice bias).
- Don't converge too early; protect divergent energy, then decide deliberately.

## Exit checklist
Group warmed up · many ideas generated against the challenge · ideas shared and clustered · shortlisted by voting + impact/effort · a few candidates chosen to prototype. Proceed to `prototyping`.
