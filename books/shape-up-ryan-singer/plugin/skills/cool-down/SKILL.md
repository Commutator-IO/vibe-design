# Cool-down

The two weeks between six-week cycles. No scheduled project work — this is deliberate slack that keeps the system healthy and the next bets good.

## What cool-down is for
- **Let the storm pass.** After a cycle, give people unstructured time to breathe, tie off loose ends, and recover. Protect it — don't fill it with a mini-cycle.
- **Fix bugs.** Handle the bugs that came up. Shape Up treats bugs as normal life, not emergencies: most can wait for a cool-down; only a true fire interrupts a cycle.
- **Explore.** Free time to try ideas, spike technical questions, or clean up code without a bet's pressure.
- **Shape the next round.** Shapers develop and refine pitches so the upcoming betting table has good options.

## Principles
- **Stay debt-free.** Don't let a cycle end with a pile of deferred cleanup that compounds. Address it here rather than carrying it.
- **Feedback needs to be shaped.** Raw customer requests and ideas aren't ready to build — they must go back through `shape-a-pitch` before they can be bet on. Cool-down is where that shaping happens.
- **Keep the slate clean.** Don't accumulate a backlog of promises during cool-down; capture ideas loosely and let the important ones resurface at betting.

## Hand-off
When cool-down ends, the betting table (`bet-the-cycle`) chooses the next bets from the pitches shaped here, and a new six-week cycle begins.

## Exit checklist
Team rested · bugs triaged and the worst fixed · exploration time taken · next pitches shaped and ready for the betting table · no debt carried forward. Loop to `bet-the-cycle`.
