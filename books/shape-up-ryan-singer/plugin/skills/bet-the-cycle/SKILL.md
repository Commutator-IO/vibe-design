# Bet the Cycle

Decide what to build in the next six weeks by making **bets**, not by grooming a backlog. Betting is a short, high-level meeting held during cool-down among senior people who can commit.

## The setup
- **Six-week cycle + two-week cool-down.** Bets fill the cycle; cool-down is for the betting decision, bug-fixing, and shaping.
- **Team and project size.** A typical team is one designer + one or two programmers per bet. Bets are sized to that team for one cycle.
- **No backlogs.** Don't maintain a master to-do of everything. Un-bet pitches are simply not scheduled; if an idea matters, it will come back.

## What a bet means
A bet is a two-way commitment:
- The team gets **uninterrupted time** — a full, protected cycle to focus, no side quests.
- In return, the work is expected to **ship within the cycle**.
- **Circuit breaker:** if it doesn't ship in six weeks, it stops by default. No automatic extension. This caps the downside and forces shaping/scoping discipline.

## Choose the bets
First, **look at where you are** — is this product/area in R&D, production, or cleanup mode? That sets the kind of bets that make sense. Then, for each candidate pitch, ask the five questions:
1. **Does the problem matter?**
2. **Is the appetite right?** (Worth the time it would take?)
3. **Is the solution attractive?** (Is the shaped approach good?)
4. **Is this the right time?** (Now, or later?)
5. **Are the right people available?** (The team it needs is free this cycle.)

Pick a small number of bets that fill the available teams. Leave everything else unbet.

## Kick off
Post a **kick-off message** for each bet: link the pitch, name the team, state the appetite and the cycle dates. Then hand over to `build-in-cycles`.

## Exit checklist
Cool-down window used for betting · candidates evaluated against the five questions · a few bets chosen to fill the teams · circuit breaker understood · kick-off messages posted. Proceed to `build-in-cycles`.
