# Friday — Test & learn

The payoff: five one-on-one interviews with real users, watched together by the whole team. By the fifth, the big patterns are unmistakable.

## Why five, watched together
- **Five is the magic number** — ~85% of problems surface in five interviews. Do all five in one day.
- **Watch together, learn together** — don't disband the team; stream to a room where everyone notes on the same grid.
- **A winner every time** — an efficient failure or a flawed success both tell you what to do next.

## The five-act interview
1. **Friendly welcome** — put them at ease; you want candid feedback and you're testing the product, not them.
2. **Context questions** — small talk, then ease into the topic to understand their world.
3. **Introduce the prototype** — some things may not work; ask them to think aloud.
4. **Tasks and nudges** — give a task, then stay quiet. Watch them figure it out. Nudge gently; ask "what are you thinking?" — never lead.
5. **Debrief** — ask them to summarize what worked, what confused them, how it compares. Thank them, give the incentive, show them out.

_Prompt to offer:_ "Write a five-act interview script testing [prototype] against these sprint questions: [list]. Include welcome, context questions, 3 realistic tasks with neutral nudges, and debrief questions. Keep every question non-leading."

## Capture and learn
As the team watches, note reactions on a grid: one column per interview, one row per flow step; green for worked, red for failed. After the fifth, find patterns across **3+ users** — those are real. Then:
- Compare what you saw against Monday's sprint questions. Which got answered?
- Decide the next step: **ship · fix and retest · pivot · stop.** Because the build was thin and disposable, any of these is cheap.

_Prompt to offer:_ "Cluster the patterns in these 5 interview notes that appear in 3+ sessions, map each back to my sprint questions, and recommend a concrete next step with reasoning."

## Exit checklist
Room + recording set up · team watching together on a shared grid · five interviews done in one day · patterns (3+ users) mapped to sprint questions · next step decided · prototype + learnings archived for the next sprint.
