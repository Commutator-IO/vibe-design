# Tuesday — Sketch (research & options)

Fill Monday's target with possible solutions. Morning: steal the best ideas that already exist. Afternoon: develop complete, competing solutions alone. Still no building.

## Morning — Lightning Demos
Survey great solutions from a range of products — competitors, adjacent industries, the user's own past attempts. ~3 minutes each. For every one, capture the single good idea worth stealing as a quick note. Remix, don't copy.

_Prompt to offer:_ "Show how 6 well-designed products solve [target moment], inside and outside my field. Name the one idea worth stealing from each and its technical approach (libraries, patterns, APIs)."

## Afternoon — the Four-Step Sketch
Enforce **work alone together** — no group brainstorm. Each participant develops a full solution privately.
1. **Notes** (20 min) — gather the goal, questions, and best demo ideas into notes.
2. **Ideas** (20 min) — privately jot rough ideas; circle the most promising.
3. **Crazy 8s** (8 min) — eight fast variations of the best idea, one minute each. Forces past the obvious first answer.
4. **Solution sketch** (30–90 min) — a three-panel storyboard of the best idea: self-explanatory, anonymous, ugly is fine, words matter, catchy title.

For a build sprint, run Claude as several distinct minds. Ask for **three genuinely different** solution sketches — different UX AND different architecture — not variations of one idea.

_Prompt to offer:_ "Give 3 meaningfully different complete solution sketches for [target moment]: title, 3-step flow, key screens, rough architecture. Not variations of one idea; ugly and concrete beats polished and vague."

## Keep recruiting
Confirm Friday's five users are on track (an hour or two today).

## Exit checklist
Lightning Demos captured · divide-or-swarm decided · Four-Step Sketch done · 3 distinct, titled solution sketches saved · users being confirmed. Then move to `wednesday-decide`.
